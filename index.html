The term bitmap comes from computer programming terminology, which means simply a bit map, a spatially mapped array of bits.

Pixel fonts, like OffBit by Power Type Foundry, are a unique category of typefaces designed around a strict grid of pixels, giving each character a modular, blocky structure. Originally developed for early digital screens and low-resolution displays, OffBit embraces this heritage while modernizing it, offering multiple stylistic variations that retain readability while allowing for expressive design choices. Its sharp, geometric forms make it ideal for both retro-inspired graphics and contemporary interfaces, demonstrating that pixel-based typography can be both functional and visually engaging. By carefully balancing the constraints of the pixel grid with thoughtful design, OffBit proves that even the smallest letters can communicate clearly, making it a versatile choice for digital interfaces, UI elements, and creative displays.
